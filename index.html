Evogen

The young association of biotechnology students SCTCE was established in 2014, with the basic objective of empowering students of biotechnology, through creation of a conductive environment for them to exress their basic basic skill sets as well as to aid them in developing new skills which could help them in fullfilling their commitment towards the society by pursuing a fullfilling career in the area of Biotechnology and to aid them in spreading the spirit of Biotechnology so as to take this vererable discipline to newer heights in the days ahead.

EVOGEN was able to successfully conduct National Science Day Celebrations, Ozone Celebrations, etc by arranging events like bio quizzes, poster presentation, expert lectures and workshops using the fund sanctioned by Kerala State Council for Science Technology and Environment (KSCTSE). We were also able to organise a National level symposium Equilibria 2016 on Advances in Biosciences & Bioengineering and Equilibria 2017 on Trends & prospects in Bio-Science and Technology towards Sustainability

Equilibria 2017

prospects in Bio-Science and Technology towards Sustainability

After the successful completion of Equilibria 2016, the second edition was held on April 2017 with the motto evolution via innovation. It proved to be a brilliant platform for students, academicians, entrepreneurs and research scholars to step into a world of scientific research through sustainable development. Active participation was witnessed with more than 200 presentations all over India.
Research articles covering up almost all areas of advances in biosciences and bioengineering were presented and amongst them, the most relevant and innovative ones were honored with the BEST PAPER and BEST POSTER AWARD. The event also flouished by experts from research and academia, addressing current developments and emerging trends. Amongst other technical events conducted, a demonstration class on Kung Fu was also organised.

Equilibria 2016

Advances in Biosciences & Bioengineering

Equilibria 2016 was a maiden attempt to pool intellectual resources from all relevant domains of bioscience research. With almost more than 200 participants from more than 30 institutions across South India, the event can be considered as a reinvigorating force to technology and biosciences in Kerala.
Research articles covering up almost all areas of advances in biosciences and bioengineering were presented and amongst them, the most relevant and innovative ones were honored with the BEST PAPER and BEST POSTER AWARD. Lectures related to the topic were dealt by scientists, entrepreneurs and academicians from the most prestigious national research institutes like CSIR, CTCRI, TBGRI etc. Apart from explorations to wide range of scientific avenues, Equilibria 2016 also brought the traditional cultural fiesta of Kerala into limelight.

The Institution

Sree Chitra Thirunal College of Engineering (SCTCE), Trivandrum, was established by the Government of Kerala in the year 1995 in memoriam of the Great Maharaja of Travancore and is affiliated to the University of Kerala with AICTE approval. The college is a realisation of a long felt need for an institution catering to a growing number of undergraduates opting for technical education.
It seeks to promote education and research in the field of technology; to accumulate effective ideas and techniques likely to promote the socio-economic welfare of the people of Kerala and be able to provide proper guidance and support to young men and women who can make these ideas work.
The management commitee is lead by the Honorable Minister for Transport and it's eminent membership includes representatives of Government of Kerala viz: -the Higher Education Secretary, Finance Secretary, Transport Secretary, Managing Director of Kerala State Road Transport Corporation.

About the department

Department of biotechnology & Biochemical engineering

Established in the year 2002, the Department of Biotechnology & Biochemical Engineering is the youngest at Sree Chitra Thirunal College of Engineering. Over the past decade, the department has been offering an undergraduate (B.Tech) programme in Biotechnology and Biochemical Engineering. The faculty of this department constitutes a unique pool with expertise in diverse subject areas like Microbiology, Biochemistry, Biotechnology and Chemical Engineering.
The department has been constantly maintaining the mindset of aligning it's resources towards the welfare of the society. In the past, it has organised several state of the art training programmes, workshops, seminars and conferences under the aegis of the TEQIP scheme (Government of India), for the benifit of faculty, students and the general public. These include:

  • in silico Biology - A course cum workshop (2005)
  • Bioinfo-Blitz - A workshop on Bioinformatics (2007)
  • Faculty development progrramme in Environmental Biotechnology (2008)
  • Faculty development programme on Advances in Nanotechnology (2009)
  • Community development programme on food production (2009)
  • Students' training programme on Bioprocess engineering (2009)
  • National conference on Advances Processing Technologies (2010)
